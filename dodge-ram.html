Dodge Ram GVM Upgrade FAQs

Common questions about upgrading your Ram

Dodge Ram GVM ratings vary significantly by model. The Ram 1500 typically has a factory GVM around 3,100-3,400kg, while the Ram 2500 and 3500 have higher ratings. Once you add accessories, payload, and towing equipment, these limits can be reached.

Any increase beyond the factory GVM requires a certified GVM upgrade completed and approved in accordance with Australian regulations. Your final upgraded GVM will depend on the specific kit used, engineering approval, and your state or territory requirements.

The cost of a Dodge Ram GVM upgrade in Australia typically ranges from $5,000 to $12,000+, depending on the model (1500/2500/3500), upgrade kit, installer, engineering requirements, and your location.

Pricing can vary significantly based on your vehicle's condition, the level of GVM increase you're seeking, and state-based compliance processes. Your chosen installer or engineer will provide a detailed quote based on your specific vehicle and requirements.

Warranty outcomes depend on Ram's warranty terms, your vehicle's age, and which components are modified. While a properly certified GVM upgrade does not automatically void your entire vehicle warranty, specific components may be affected.

You should confirm any warranty implications directly with Ram/Stellantis and your chosen provider before proceeding. Warranty coverage is determined by the manufacturer and installer, not by this website or any referral platform.

A Dodge Ram GVM upgrade typically includes upgraded heavy-duty suspension components such as springs and shock absorbers, along with an engineering assessment, certification, and modification plate where required.

Exact inclusions vary depending on the upgrade kit, installer, and your state or territory regulations. You should confirm the full scope of work, certification pathway, and registration requirements with your chosen provider before proceeding.

Most Dodge Ram GVM upgrades are completed within 2–4 weeks from initial consultation to final certification. Timeframes can vary based on parts availability, installer schedules, engineering inspections, and registration authority processing times.

Your installer will be best placed to confirm an accurate timeline once your vehicle details and upgrade scope are known.

⚠️ Important note

GVM upgrade rules, costs, certification processes, insurance implications, and warranty outcomes vary by state, vehicle condition, installer, and intended use. You are responsible for confirming compliance with your installer, engineer, insurer, and registration authority before proceeding.
