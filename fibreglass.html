Fibreglass FAQs

Common questions about fibreglass pool resurfacing

Fibreglass pool resurfacing involves applying multiple layers of fibreglass and gelcoat to create a smooth, waterproof surface. It can be applied to both concrete and existing fibreglass pools, providing excellent durability and a low-maintenance finish.

Fibreglass pool resurfacing typically costs between $12,000 and $25,000 depending on pool size, condition, and the number of layers required. This is a mid-to-premium option that offers excellent value for its longevity and low maintenance costs.

Yes, fibreglass can be applied to concrete pools. The concrete surface is prepared, then multiple layers of fibreglass are applied to create a smooth, waterproof finish. This is a popular option for renovating older concrete pools.

A quality fibreglass resurfacing typically lasts 20+ years with proper care. The smooth, non-porous surface is resistant to algae, staining, and chemical damage, contributing to its longevity.

Fibreglass gelcoats are available in a wide range of colours, from classic blues and whites to more modern greys and greens. Your specialist can show you colour samples to find the perfect match for your backyard.
