Common Questions

Everything you need to know about pool resurfacing

Pool resurfacing costs vary based on pool size, current condition, and chosen finish. In Australia, standard resurfacing typically costs between $8,500 and $16,000, while premium finishes like quartz aggregate or full tiling can exceed $20,000+.
Popular resurfacing options include pebblecrete (natural aggregate finish), fibreglass coating (smooth, waterproof layer), pool tiling (ceramic or mosaic), and quartz aggregate (premium crystal finish). Each offers different aesthetics, durability, and price points.
The typical timeline is 1-2 weeks from start to finish. This includes draining the pool, surface preparation, material application, and curing time. Weather conditions and pool size can affect the timeline.
With quality materials and proper maintenance, pool resurfacing can last 15-25 years. Pebblecrete typically lasts 15-20 years, fibreglass 20+ years, and quality tiling can last even longer with proper care.
Common signs include rough or abrasive surfaces that snag swimwear, visible cracks or crazing, persistent staining that won't clean, chalky deposits, blistering or peeling (fibreglass), loose pebbles (pebblecrete), and needing more chemicals to maintain balance. The best time to resurface is autumn or winter so your pool is ready for summer.
