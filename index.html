Common Questions

Everything you need to know about GVM upgrades

GVM upgrade costs vary depending on your vehicle, the upgrade kit required, and your location. Typical costs range from $2,000 to $8,000 including parts, installation, and certification. Your local installer will provide a detailed quote based on your specific requirements.
Yes, GVM upgrades are legal in Australia when performed correctly with approved components and certified by an engineer. The upgrade must be registered with your state's transport authority. Our network installers ensure full compliance with all regulations.
The typical timeline is 2-4 weeks from initial consultation to completion. This includes assessment, ordering parts, installation (usually 1-2 days), and engineering certification. Timelines may vary based on parts availability and installer schedules.
Most insurance companies will continue to cover your vehicle after a properly certified GVM upgrade. You should notify your insurer of the modification. A compliant upgrade with engineering certification typically doesn't negatively impact your coverage.
ADR stands for Australian Design Rules — the national standards for vehicle safety, emissions, and anti-theft compliance. An ADR Compliant GVM upgrade means the modification meets these federal standards and has been certified by an approved engineer.
