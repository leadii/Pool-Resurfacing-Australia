Pebblecrete FAQs

Common questions about pebblecrete pool resurfacing

Pebblecrete is a pool finish made from natural pebbles mixed with cement. It creates a durable, slip-resistant surface that's available in various colours and textures. It's one of the most popular pool resurfacing options in Australia due to its natural appearance and longevity.

Pebblecrete pool resurfacing typically costs between $10,000 and $18,000 depending on pool size, condition, and chosen pebble type. Smaller pools may cost less, while larger or more complex pools can exceed this range. Get free quotes from local specialists to get an accurate price for your pool.

A well-maintained pebblecrete finish typically lasts 15-20 years. Proper water chemistry, regular cleaning, and avoiding harsh chemicals will help maximise the lifespan of your pebblecrete surface.

Pebblecrete is available in a wide range of colours and textures, from natural earth tones to vibrant blues and greens. Popular options include Coral Sea, Lagoon Blue, Daintree, and various sand and cream tones. Your specialist can show you samples to match your backyard aesthetic.

The typical timeline for pebblecrete resurfacing is 1-2 weeks. This includes draining the pool, surface preparation, applying the pebblecrete, curing time, and refilling. Weather conditions can affect the timeline.
