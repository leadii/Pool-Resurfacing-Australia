Pool Tiling FAQs

Common questions about pool tiling

Options include ceramic tiles, porcelain tiles, glass mosaic tiles, and natural stone tiles. Each offers different aesthetics, durability, and price points. Glass mosaics are popular for their shimmer, while porcelain offers excellent durability.

Pool tiling typically costs between $15,000 and $30,000+ depending on pool size, tile type, and design complexity. Glass mosaic tiles are at the premium end, while ceramic tiles offer a more affordable option.

Quality pool tiles can last 25+ years with proper care. The grout may need resealing every 5-10 years, but the tiles themselves are extremely durable and resistant to chemicals, UV, and wear.

Pool tiling works best on concrete pools. Fibreglass pools can have waterline tiles but typically aren't fully tiled. Your specialist will assess your pool and recommend the best approach for your situation.

A full pool tiling project typically takes 2-4 weeks, depending on pool size and tile complexity. This includes surface preparation, tile installation, grouting, and curing time.
