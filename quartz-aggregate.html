Quartz Aggregate FAQs

Common questions about quartz aggregate resurfacing

Quartz aggregate is a premium pool finish that combines cement with quartz crystals and other aggregates. The exposed quartz creates a beautiful sparkle effect in sunlight, making it a popular choice for luxury pools. It's similar to pebblecrete but uses polished quartz for a more refined finish.

Quartz aggregate pool resurfacing typically costs between $18,000 and $35,000+ depending on pool size and the specific aggregate blend chosen. It's a premium option that offers exceptional durability and aesthetics, making it worth the investment for many homeowners.

A quality quartz aggregate finish typically lasts 20-25 years with proper maintenance. The quartz crystals are extremely hard and resistant to chemicals, staining, and wear, contributing to its exceptional longevity.

Quartz aggregate is available in a range of colours from classic blues and aquas to sophisticated greys, creams, and earth tones. The quartz crystals add a sparkle effect to whichever base colour you choose. Your specialist can show you samples.

Yes, quartz aggregate finishes are polished smooth, making them comfortable underfoot. Unlike some pebblecrete finishes, quartz aggregate doesn't have rough edges. It's slip-resistant when wet while still being gentle on feet.
